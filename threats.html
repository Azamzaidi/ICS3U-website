Threats to be noted

  • Malware
  • Phishing
  • Hacking

Types of Malware

  • Malware is a harmful software designed to damage, steal, or spy on a computer system.
  • Virus - attaches to files and spreads when opened.
  • Worm - spreads automatically across networks.
  • Trojan Horse - disguised as legit software to trick you.
  • Ransomware - locks your files and demands payment.
  • Spyware - secretly monitors your activity and steals data.
  • Scareware - fake alerts that push you to install malware.
  • How Malware gets into Your Device
  • Downloading cracked or unofficial apps/software.
  • Clicking suspicious links in messages, emails, or websites.
  • Opening unknown email attachments or files.
  • Visiting fake or unsafe websites.
  • Using public Wi‑Fi without protection.
  • Falling for phishing texts or scam messages.
  • Installing browser extensions from untrusted sources.
  • Plugging in unknown USB drives.
  • Ignoring software updates and security patches.
  • Allowing excessive app permissions or unknown installers.

Six Major types of Phishing

  • Email Phishing - Fake emails pretending to be trusted companies.
  • Spear Phishing - Targeted phishing aimed at a specific person.
  • Whaling - Phishing attacks targeting executives or high level officials.
  • Vishing - Phishing through phone calls (voice scams)
  • Clone Phishing - Copying a real email and replacing links with malicious ones.
  • Clean and Effective ways to avoid all types of Phishing
  • Always verify the sender by checking the real email address, phone number, or website before responding.
  • Never click links or open attachments from unknown or unexpected messages.
  • Use strong, unique passwords with 2FA so attackers can’t access your accounts even if you slip.
  • Keep your devices, browsers, and security software updated to block phishing attempts.
  • Slow down, double‑check the message, and contact the company directly if anything feels urgent or suspicious.

Basic Hacking Methods

  • Password Attack: Hackers guess or steal your passwords to break in.
  • Malware Installation: They slip malware onto your device to gain control.
  • Exploiting Software Vulnerabilities: They use flaws in software to sneak in.
  • Wi‑Fi Hacking: They break into unsecured networks to intercept data.
  • Social Engineering: They manipulate people to give up information.
  • How to Avoid These Hacking Methods
  • Use unique passwords and enable 2FA to block password‑guessing attacks.
  • Install apps only from trusted sources and keep antivirus active to prevent malware installation.
  • Update your software regularly so hackers can’t exploit old vulnerabilities.
  • Use secure, password‑protected Wi‑Fi and avoid public networks for sensitive tasks.
  • Stay alert to manipulation, verify requests, and never share personal info with strangers.
